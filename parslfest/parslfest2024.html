ParslFest 2024 - Annual Community Gathering (Hybrid)

We invite you to join us for ParslFest 2024 on September 26–27. This annual community event brings together researchers, developers, and cyberinfrastructure experts worldwide to discuss experiences using and developing Parsl and Globus Compute - a federated function-as-a-service platform related to Parsl.

Registration and Call for Lightning Talks

Registration is free and open! We encourage you to share your work and experience using Parsl and/or Globus Compute with the community in a lightning talk. Presentation recordings will be posted to our site after the event and is an excellent opportunity to raise awareness of your work/project. Please consider signing up for a lighting talk when registering.

Event Format and Travel Support

Join us in person in Chicago, IL or virtually. Due to limited funding, we can only offer partial travel support to speakers. For more information about travel support for speakers, please message Parsl Community Manager Sophie Bui on Slack.

Location (for in-person component)

ParslFest will be held in the Orange & Blue Conference Room at the Illini Center located at:
200 S. Wacker Drive, 19th Floor
Chicago, Illinois, 60606

Driving Directions

From the west, follow the Eisenhower Expressway (290) east into town, where it becomes Congress Avenue. Exit Wacker Drive and follow the signs that read Upper Wacker Drive. You will be driving north. The Illini Center will be on your left just before Adams Street. From the south, follow the Kennedy Expressway (90/94) west and exit on Monroe Street. Turn right (east) on Monroe and follow it to Wacker Drive and turn right (south). The Illini Center will be on your right just after Adams Street. From the north, follow the Kennedy Expressway (90/94) south and exit on Jackson Street. Turn left (east) on Jackson and follow it to Wacker Drive and turn left (north). The Illini Center will be on your left just before Adams Street.

Public Transportation

The Center is located just two blocks from the Quincy brown line stop on the CTA ‘L’ train and near several bus stops. For Metra users, Union Station is just across the river and Ogilvie Transportation Center is two blocks away. Union Station also services Amtrak trains, the Lincolnland Express and Megabus.

  • CTA ‘L’ Train and Bus: transitchicago.com
  • Metra: metrarail.com
  • Amtrak: amtrak.com

Event Program

Below is a high-level overview of our program agenda. More details on sessions, talks, and event offerings will be added when available. Meals will be provided on both days.

  • Thursday, September 26: Main talks and workshops will occur in the afternoon. In the evening, we have an in-person social activity planned from 6–8 p.m. CST at Flight Club Chicago (an RSVP form will be sent to in-person registrants later).
  • Friday, September 27: Main talks and workshops begin in the morning. The hackathon session (topic TBD based on the registration poll) will occur in the afternoon.
